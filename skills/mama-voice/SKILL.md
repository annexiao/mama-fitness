# Mama site voice

This site is written **for one reader: 妈妈** (an older Chinese parent, not a fitness or
medical person). Every line should feel like a caring, plain-spoken explanation from
someone who wants her to move well and stay safe. Match this voice exactly.

## Who she is (audience)

- Older Chinese woman, reads Chinese only. **No English jargon**: plain Chinese throughout.
- Not technical, not a gym person. Has never learned words like 筋膜 / 髌骨 / 背屈.
- Cares about: not getting hurt, staying strong enough to climb / travel / carry things,
  knees and back not hurting. Wants to understand *why*, not memorize a plan.
- Reads on a phone. Short paragraphs, big enough ideas per line.

## The 10 rules of the voice

1. **Address her as 你; use 我们 for shared practice.** "你先按 12 到 15 次来试" (instructing).
   "我们主要练 6 到 12 次" (what we do together). This warmth is load-bearing.

2. **Plainest possible words. Gloss every technical term inline, in the same breath.**
   Never let a jargon word stand alone. Always: `筋膜` → 「脚底的筋（筋膜）」, `腰椎` →
   「腰椎（下背的脊柱）」, `髌骨` → 「膝盖骨」, `背屈` → 「脚踝往上勾」. If you can't gloss
   it simply, don't use it.

3. **Short sentences, one idea each.** Break long thoughts with 。 not 、chains. If a
   sentence needs two commas to breathe, it's probably two sentences.

4. **Explain WHY before HOW.** Lead with what it's for and what it does for her body, then
   the concrete steps. Motivation first makes the instruction stick.

5. **Always give concrete numbers, written with 到 (never a dash).** "一次踩 2 到 3 分钟",
   "每个动作停 20 到 30 秒", "一周 2 到 3 次". Numbers reassure and make it doable.
   (Avoid em-dash / en-dash for ranges: 到 reads gentler and clearer.)

6. **Be honest. Never oversell.** If something only helps a little, say so plainly
   ("光按脚底帮助就有限了"). If the real fix is elsewhere, point there ("真正护住膝盖的，
   还是把臀腿的力量练起来"). Honesty is the site's whole tone.

7. **Use homely analogies.** "力量和有氧就像两条腿，缺一条都走不稳。" "脚底是全身的地基。"
   A picture she can hold beats an abstract explanation.

8. **Safety cautions are specific and kind.** Tell her exactly when it's normal, when to
   stop, and when to ask a doctor: "酸胀是正常、一有刺痛就停、隔天再踩。" "有骨质疏松或者
   急性疼痛，先问医生。" Never scold; never hand-wave.

9. **Cross-reference other pages with 「」.** "（泡沫轴怎么用，看「器械 · 泡沫轴」页里的
   讲解视频。）"

10. **Warm, encouraging close.** End on "能坚持最重要", not on a warning. The recurring
    signature line: **"哪次累了就练轻一点，能长期坚持最重要。"** / "比一两天拼命更重要。"

## Formatting

- Paragraphs separated by a blank line (the site renders them with `white-space:pre-line`).
- No em-dash (—) or en-dash (–) anywhere. Use ，：（）。 and 到 for ranges.
- No Markdown bold/headers inside article bodies — just plain prose paragraphs.
- Light, natural tone. No exclamation-mark hype, no emoji inside body copy (emoji are only
  the little group/tool icons in the data, e.g. `icon: 🏋️`).

## Litmus test before shipping any line

Read it out loud as if you were explaining it to your own mom over the phone. If a word
would make her ask "那是什么意思？", or if it sounds like a coach or a textbook instead of
a caring person, rewrite it.
